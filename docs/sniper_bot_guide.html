Egon Sniper Bot

RSI mean-reversion scalping with limit order pre-placement

How It Works

The sniper bot exploits a simple market behavior: when RSI reaches extreme levels (oversold/overbought), price tends to snap back toward the mean. Instead of waiting for the candle to close to confirm the signal, the bot pre-calculates what price would push RSI to those levels and places limit orders there in advance.

Price Action with RSI Sniper: $2,345 ─────────────────────── RSI 65 (sell signal zone) \ Limit SELL placed here (offset 10 -> RSI 75 level) \ /\ /\ \ / \ / \ ← Normal oscillation (profitable) \/ \/ \ $2,335 ─────────────── RSI 50 (mean - where exits happen) \ \ $2,325 ─────────────────── RSI 35 (buy signal zone) Limit BUY placed here (offset 10 -> RSI 25 level)

The Two Entry Mechanisms

  1. Sniper Limit Order (primary) — placed at a price that would push RSI even deeper than the entry threshold. Catches intra-candle wicks. No spread cost (limit order provides liquidity).
  2. Fallback Market Order — if the limit didn't fill and the candle closes with RSI past the threshold, enters at market price. Never misses a valid signal.

Exit Logic: Mean Reversion

The exit target is configurable via exit_rsi (default: 50). The position closes when RSI returns to this level. With adaptive exit enabled, the target shifts based on trend:

  • With-trend (e.g., long in an uptrend): exits at exit_rsi + shift (e.g., 55) — lets winners run
  • Against-trend (e.g., long in a downtrend): exits at exit_rsi - shift (e.g., 45) — gets out earlier
  • Neutral/sideways: exits at the base exit_rsi (e.g., 50)

There are no separate "safety exit" thresholds. The mean-revert exit is the only RSI-based exit. The trailing stop and breakeven system handle downside protection.

Risk-based sizing (recommended): If your account is $10,000, risk is 2%, and your stop loss is $2.50 away from entry:
lots = ($10,000 x 0.02) / ($2.50 x 100 oz) = 0.80 lots
If the stop gets hit, you lose exactly $200 (2% of account). Wider stops = smaller position. Tighter stops = larger position. Your risk stays constant regardless of volatility.

effective_risk = risk_per_trade_pct x (median_atr / current_atr) ^ atr_damping

Trailing Stop

The trailing stop system protects profits in two stages:

Stage 1: BEFORE breakeven (position is young, not yet profitable enough) ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── Current Price ── $2,342.50 Entry ────────── $2,340.00 Stop Loss ────── $2,338.00 (trailing 1.5 ATR behind price, wide) The SL trails upward as price rises, but stays 1.5 ATR away. Wide trail avoids getting stopped out by normal noise. Stage 2: AFTER breakeven triggers (profit exceeded 0.7 ATR) ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── Current Price ── $2,343.80 Entry ────────── $2,340.00 SL moved to ──── $2,340.20 (breakeven + small offset for spread) Now trailing ─── $2,342.30 (1.0 ATR behind price, tighter) Once breakeven fires, the trail tightens. You can't lose money on this trade anymore. The tighter trail locks in more profit.

Volatility Guard

Automatically pauses entries when ATR spikes beyond normal levels (breakout detection). Prevents entering during high-volatility events where mean reversion is unreliable.

FieldGUI LabelDescriptionExample
vg_enabledVol Guard EnabledMaster toggle. False = no volatility guard.True
vg_atr_spike_multiplierSpike Threshold (x)Pause when current ATR exceeds median ATR times this value. 2.5 = pause when ATR is 2.5x its recent typical level.2.5
vg_cooldown_minutesCooldown (min)Minimum minutes to stay paused before checking if ATR has calmed down.15

How it works: The guard compares current ATR to the median ATR of the last ~100 bars. If ATR spikes to 2.5x its normal level (likely a breakout or news event), entries are paused. After the cooldown period, it checks if ATR has dropped below 1.5x median. If so, normal trading resumes. If ATR is still elevated, it stays paused.

When It Works Well

  • Trending markets with pullbacks — larger swings mean deeper RSI dips that bounce harder. The sniper catches pullbacks within the trend for significant profit per trade.
  • Active hours (London + NY sessions) — enough volume and participation for clean mean reversion behavior.
  • ATR > $1.50 on M5 — enough movement per candle to cover the $0.30 spread + commission.

When It Struggles

  • Breakouts through key support/resistance — RSI fires "oversold" but price keeps going because a structural level broke. The Breakout Shield (see below) detects this and blocks re-entry until the breakout is over.
  • Dead/quiet markets (Asian session) — waves are too small, spread eats the profit. The Market Rhythm module (see below) detects dead markets and pauses entries.
  • Trending without pullbacks — RSI stays on one side of 50 for extended periods. The Market Rhythm module classifies this as "trending" and blocks RSI entries.
  • News events — unpredictable spikes that bypass normal mean reversion. Use the schedule for known events; the volatility guard catches unexpected ones.
The biggest risk is catching a falling knife during a breakout: RSI shows oversold, you buy, but price drops another $10 because a major level just broke. Protections: (1) the trailing stop limits the loss, (2) the volatility guard pauses during ATR spikes, (3) risk_pct sizing ensures each loss is bounded to X% of account, (4) the Breakout Shield blocks re-entry after a stop-loss until market analysis confirms the breakout is over.

Market Rhythm Analyzer

The rhythm module continuously analyzes RSI cycle characteristics to determine whether the market is suitable for RSI-based swing trading. It answers three questions:

  1. Is the market oscillating with regular RSI swings? (regime detection)
  2. Are the swings large enough to trade profitably? (amplitude check)
  3. Where should limit orders actually be placed? (support-aware sniper placement)

How It Works

RSI Cycle Detection (tracking midline crossings): RSI 70 ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ * ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ * ─ ─ ─ ─ ← Extremes (amplitude) 60 / \ / \ 50 ── X ── /─── X ── ── /─── X ── ── X ─ ← Midline crossings (X) 40 / \ / \ / \ / 30 ─*─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ * ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ * ─ ─ ← Extremes |--- 12 bars ---|--- 13 bars --| ← Half-cycle length |----------- 25 bars ----------| ← Full cycle

The module tracks RSI crossings of the 50 midline and measures:

  • Half-cycle bars — median bars between consecutive 50-crossings
  • Amplitude — how far RSI goes from 50 at each extreme (in RSI points and dollars)
  • Stability — how consistent the cycle lengths are (low variance = stable = predictable)

Dynamic Mode Adjustments

In dynamic mode, the rhythm module continuously adjusts these parameters:

ParameterAdaptation Logic
Position sizingScaled down proportionally when swing amplitude is low relative to target profit. Small swings = smaller positions.
SL / trail distanceTightened when cycles are stable and confidence is high. Widened when cycles are irregular (gives more room to survive noise).
Sniper offsetInstead of fixed RSI offset, places limit orders at the observed typical extreme depth. If recent swings only reach RSI 32, it places there — not at RSI 25 which would only catch breakouts.
Breakeven triggerFaster breakeven in high-amplitude swings (expect quick moves). Slower breakeven in low-amplitude (give more room).

Support-Aware Sniper Placement

When rhythm_support_aware_sniper: true, the module cross-references sniper limit order levels against detected support/resistance (liquidity zones):

Without support-aware: With support-aware: Current: $2,340 Current: $2,340 RSI buy target: $2,330 (RSI 25) RSI buy target: $2,330 (RSI 25) Demand zone: $2,332-$2,335 Limit BUY @ $2,330 Limit BUY @ $2,335 (capped at zone top) ↓ ↓ If $2,332 is strong support, Buy limit stays ABOVE support. placing below it means you're You catch the bounce OFF support, catching a BREAK of support not the break THROUGH it. (likely a breakout = bad entry)
Why this matters: Placing buy limits below demand zones means you only fill if support breaks — which is exactly when mean reversion fails. By capping at the zone edge, the bot catches swing bounces off support (what it's designed for) and avoids breakout traps.

Multi-Timeframe Context

The rhythm module uses higher timeframes for confirmation:

  • Primary TF (M5): cycle detection, amplitude measurement, all entry decisions
  • HTF (M15 for M5 bot): regime confirmation — if M15 shows strong trend while M5 still oscillates, it's a warning that swings are counter-trend pullbacks
  • H1: macro context only — used by the breakout shield for trend direction

Breakout Shield

The breakout shield provides market-aware re-entry protection after any stop-loss exit. Unlike a simple cooldown timer, the shield analyzes the market to determine whether a structural breakout is in progress and blocks re-entry until evidence confirms the breakout is over (or has failed).

Why It Exists

The sniper bot's most damaging failure mode: entering at "oversold" RSI, getting stopped out by a breakout, then immediately re-entering at the next oversold level and getting stopped again. Three consecutive stop-losses in a breakout can wipe out 20+ profitable trades. The shield prevents this cascade.

How It Works

Timeline of a breakout scenario WITHOUT shield: Entry 1 (RSI oversold) ─── SL hit after 2 bars ─── Loss $50 Entry 2 (RSI oversold) ─── SL hit after 1 bar ──── Loss $50 Entry 3 (RSI oversold) ─── SL hit after 3 bars ─── Loss $50 Total: -$150 Same scenario WITH shield: Entry 1 (RSI oversold) ─── SL hit after 2 bars ─── Loss $50 Shield ACTIVATED (rapid SL + breakout detected) ... 8 bars pass, price stabilizes, RSI crosses back above 50 ... Shield LIFTED (normalization signal: rsi_normalize) Entry 2 (RSI oversold again) ─── normal swing ──── Profit $30 Total: -$20

Post-SL Analysis (happens immediately after any stop-loss)

When a stop-loss is hit, the shield evaluates three factors:

  1. Position duration: How many candles did the position last before hitting SL?
    • < 3 bars (M5): "rapid SL" — price moved against you immediately = momentum, not a swing
    • > 10 bars: normal SL — the position had time to develop, just got caught by a deeper-than-expected swing
  2. Breakout level proximity: Was the SL price near a structural high/low? Did price break through it?
    • If price broke through a 20-bar support/resistance level = structural breakout in progress
  3. Higher timeframe alignment: Do M15 and H1 agree with the direction that stopped you?
    • If your long got stopped AND M15 RSI is below 40 AND H1 EMA shows downtrend = macro is against you

Post-Shield Sizing

After the shield lifts, the next N trades (default: 2) use reduced position size. This acknowledges that the first trade back after a breakout event carries higher uncertainty:

Shield lifts after breakout: Trade 1 (post-shield): 50% normal size, 20% wider SL ← cautious Trade 2 (post-shield): 50% normal size, 20% wider SL ← still cautious Trade 3: back to normal size and normal SL

Tuning Tips

  • More trades, lower quality: Widen rsi_buy/rsi_sell (e.g., 40/60) and reduce sniper_rsi_offset (e.g., 5). Enters more often but on weaker signals.
  • Fewer trades, higher quality: Tighten rsi_buy/rsi_sell (e.g., 30/70) and increase offset (e.g., 15). Only catches the deepest oversold/overbought extremes.
  • Let winners run longer: Increase exit_rsi_long (e.g., 58 means you hold until RSI rises further past neutral).
  • Tighter risk: Reduce risk_per_trade_pct (e.g., 0.01 = 1%). Or reduce atr_multiplier for a tighter stop (but more frequent stops).
  • M1 vs M5 vs M15: Faster timeframes = more trades, smaller moves, tighter offsets (7 for M1). Slower = fewer but larger trades, wider offsets (12 for M15).

Rhythm Tuning

  • Start with "gated" mode — safest. Watch the logs for regime changes. If the bot is pausing too often (market is fine but regime says "trending"), increase rhythm_max_cycle_bars.
  • Switch to "dynamic" when confident — dynamic mode adjusts sizing and SL automatically. Good for set-and-forget operation. Watch the sizing_scale in GUI to see if it's scaling down too aggressively.
  • Disable support-aware sniper if fills are too rare — rhythm_support_aware_sniper: false lets limit orders be placed deeper (more aggressive but fills are better prices).

Shield Tuning

  • Keep enabled — the shield is the primary defense against consecutive SL cascades during breakouts. There's no good reason to disable it.
  • Adjust rapid_sl_candles by timeframe: M1 = 6, M5 = 3, M15 = 2. A "rapid" SL on M15 (2 bars = 30 minutes) means something very different than on M1 (2 bars = 2 minutes).
  • Increase reduced_size_trades if breakouts are frequent — set to 3-4 for aggressive markets that break often (e.g., during news-heavy weeks).
  • Increase reduced_size_factor for less caution — 0.7 means you re-enter at 70% size (less conservative than the default 50%).
