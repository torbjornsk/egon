Egon Sniper Bot

RSI mean-reversion scalping with limit order pre-placement

How It Works

The sniper bot exploits a simple market behavior: when RSI reaches extreme levels (oversold/overbought), price tends to snap back toward the mean. Instead of waiting for the candle to close to confirm the signal, the bot pre-calculates what price would push RSI to those levels and places limit orders there in advance.

Price Action with RSI Sniper: $2,345 ─────────────────────── RSI 65 (sell signal zone) \ Limit SELL placed here \ /\ /\ \ / \ / \ ← Normal oscillation (profitable) \/ \/ \ $2,335 ─────────────── RSI 50 (mean - where exits happen) \ \ $2,325 ─────────────────── RSI 35 (buy signal zone) Limit BUY placed here

The Two Entry Mechanisms

  1. Sniper Limit Order (primary) — placed at a price that would push RSI even deeper than the entry threshold. Catches intra-candle wicks. No spread cost (limit order provides liquidity).
  2. Fallback Market Order — if the limit didn't fill and the candle closes with RSI past the threshold, enters at market price. Never misses a valid signal.

Exit Logic: Mean Reversion

The primary exit is "exit at mean" — close the position when RSI returns to ~50 (neutral). This is adaptive:

  • With-trend (e.g., long in an uptrend): exits at RSI 55 — lets winners run slightly longer
  • Against-trend (e.g., long in a downtrend): exits at RSI 45 — gets out earlier to protect profit
  • Neutral/sideways: exits at RSI 50

There are also safety exits (RSI > 65 for longs, RSI < 35 for shorts) that force-close if the mean revert exit somehow missed.

Risk-based sizing explained: If your account is $10,000, risk is 2%, and your stop loss is $2.50 away from entry, the bot calculates:
lots = ($10,000 x 0.02) / ($2.50 x 100 oz) = 0.80 lots
If the stop gets hit, you lose exactly $200 (2% of account). Wider stops = smaller position. Tighter stops = larger position.

Breakeven & Trailing Stop

The trailing stop system has two stages:

Stage 1: BEFORE breakeven ───────────────────────── Entry ──────── $2,340.00 Initial SL ── $2,335.62 (ATR x 1.75 below entry) Trail ──────── 1.5 ATR behind current price (wide, gives room) Stage 2: AFTER breakeven triggers (profit > 0.7 ATR) ────────────────────────────────────────────────────── Entry ──────── $2,340.00 SL moved to ── $2,340.20 (entry + small offset to cover spread) Trail ──────── 1.0 ATR behind current price (tighter, locks in profit)

When It Works Well

  • Trending markets with pullbacks — larger swings mean deeper RSI dips that bounce harder. More profit per trade.
  • Active hours (London + NY sessions) — enough volume for clean mean reversion.
  • ATR > $1.50 — enough movement to cover spread + commission.

When It Struggles

  • Breakouts through key levels — RSI fires "oversold" but price just keeps going because a structural support broke.
  • Dead/quiet markets — waves too small, spread eats the profit.
  • News events — unpredictable spikes that bypass normal mean reversion.
The biggest risk is catching a falling knife during a breakout. The volatility guard and schedule help, but a future supervisor with support/resistance awareness will be the proper fix.
